About

Our story

READ Bhutan is a nonprofit organization focused on literacy and education in Bhutan. They build community libraries and resource centers across the country.

The mission
Literacy changes everything

Their mission is rooted in the belief that literacy is the foundation for everything else, and that every child deserves the chance to read, write, and learn in a space built for them.

Through their network of READ Centers, they work to strengthen literacy skills, promote a love of reading, and open doors to education in communities that might not otherwise be accessible to them.

Our club partners with the READ center in Bhutan to support that work. Every Sunday, we hop on a call with the kids at these Centers. These weekly calls are the heart of what we do.

What READ does
Every Center has

A Library

Books, resources, and reading programs run in partnership with the Ministry of Education.

A Computer Room

Teaching digital skills to students and community members.

Women's Programs

Health classes, livelihood skills, leadership, and civic participation programs.

Community Radio

Elevating community voices through broadcasting.

Global reach
READ Global's network

READ Global operates 139 community centers across Nepal, India, and Bhutan, providing access to over 2.52 million villagers in 1,034 villages.

In 2025, READ Global was honored as a Library of Congress Successful Practices Honoree for their work creating community library and resource centers that transform rural communities.

Programs

Here's how we show up

From weekly reading calls to curriculum development, we're working to expand opportunities for young learners in Bhutan.

Core activity
Weekly reading calls

Every Sunday, we hop on a call with the kids at these Centers. These weekly calls are the heart of what we do. We connect with young learners, hear their stories, help with reading and writing, and build real relationships with them.

Recently, some members of our club had the chance to visit the READ Center in person through the Interim trip to Bhutan. Walking into the library, meeting the kids face to face, watching the facilitators pour everything they had into these programs — it made the work feel urgent and personal.

Why this trip matters
Learning objectives

Cultural Awareness

Students immerse themselves in Bhutanese traditions and values, understanding and respecting perspectives different from their own.

Teamwork & Problem-Solving

Working alongside local children and peers, students practice finding creative solutions and supporting one another in a new environment.

Empathy & Responsibility

Connecting with children from diverse backgrounds builds empathy and strengthens a sense of global citizenship.

Community & Sustainability

Seeing how community-led projects like the READ Center make a lasting difference, inspiring students to think about their own role in creating positive change.

The journey
Day by day

Day 1 — Arrival in Bhutan

Fly into Paro, one of the most dramatic airport approaches in the world. Transfer to Thimphu (2,300m), the capital. Visit the Memorial Chorten, Big Buddha, and the Takin Preserve.

Day 2 — Dochula Pass & Lungchutse Hike

Drive to Dochula Pass and hike 4–5 hours through rhododendron and hemlock forest to Lungchutse Temple at 3,600m. Views of the eastern Himalayas on a clear day. Cultural sightseeing around Thimphu in the afternoon.

Day 3 — Service Day: Meet & Greet

Arrive at the Jungshina READ Center. Presentation from READ Bhutan, then a futsal friendship match — SAS students and local primary students mixed into teams for a 40–50 minute game. Building comfort, teamwork, and friendship.

Day 4 — Service Day: Reading & Storytelling

Morning storytelling exchange — SAS students read picture books, local students share a folktale, then draw scenes from each other's stories. Buddy reading sessions in pairs. Afternoon book-making workshop where small groups write and illustrate their own storybooks.

Day 5 — Service Day: Crafts & Farewell

Library outreach or puppet-making workshop with local partners. Evening group reflection and gratitude circle — students exchange postcards between Singapore and Bhutan. Share what they learned, enjoyed, and how they'll stay connected.

Day 6 — Transfer to Paro

Drive to Paro valley (2,400m). Visit Paro Rinpung Dzong. Try traditional Bhutanese calligraphy and sand mandala making.

Day 7 — Tiger's Nest Hike

The main event: a moderate-to-strenuous 5–6 hour hike to Paro Taktshang, the iconic Tiger's Nest monastery clinging to a cliff at 3,120m. The most popular travel attraction in Bhutan. Farewell dinner in town.

Day 8 — Departure

Drive to the airport. Bid Bhutan, your guide, and driver farewell. Begin the journey back to Singapore, changed.
